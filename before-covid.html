Before Covid-19

Contributing factors

Many external factors enabled the Covid-19 propagation in different areas of Europe, making them more exposed than others to quick infection of citizens. But what are the secret paths that affected the contagion the most? Click the buttons at the bottom of the page to explore them!

Contributing factors


To perform our analysis, we clustered European regions in categories of high and low risk, in order to understand why a region belongs to a specific category on the basis of the contributing factors. In particular, as target variable we took the density of COVID-19 cases of each NUTS2 region, displayed below. Together with all the contributing factors, this data allowed us to perform our analysis and understand why certain regions, like Lombardy, have a higher risk of COVID-19 cases. Feel free to explore them all, and use the interactive visualisation to further investigate the contributing factors!

Density of COVID-19 cases

The map shows the density of COVID-19 cases normalised over 100.000 inhabitants, between August 2020 and February 2021, what it is considered the second wave of the pandemic. As the graph shows, Lombardy, together with several regions in the north of Italy, witnessed a high number of cases, together with some part of Spain and Belgium.

Education


The role of schools in the Covid19 spread has been heavily debated. Experts argued that such environment was a perfect vehicle of diffusion since many students attend lectures together in a closed space for a long time, despite masks. For this reason governors were obliged to switch from physical to remote education every time the pandemic context was worsening. We decided to investigate the most relevant Education variables available for the european landscape: scroll down and discover them!

Early leavers from education and training

It indicates the percentage of young people (between 18 and 24 years old) who left school, university or training classes after having started them, over the total number of people who joined schools, universities and training. As the graph shows, the highest percentages of early leavers are diffused in impoverished and rural areas, such as Romania, Bulgaria, the south-west of Spain and in the meridional regions of Italy.

Students enrolled in tertiary education

It is the total number of students (independent from sex and age) enrolled in tertiary schools education. The distribution is quite homogeneous across the whole Europe, except for some exceptions like Luxembourg and the northern part of Sweden, due to the low local number of people.

Partecipation rate in education and training

It represents the percentage of people between 25 and 64 years old who, in the last 4 weeks, has participated in educational and training activities. In continental Europe the pattern is homogeneous, whereas Nederland and in particular Sweden present much stronger participation in education than the average.

Young people who are unemployed and not in training

It indicates the percentage of people from 15 to 24 who are neither studying nor working over the total number of people of that age. The map reflects the strong inequality between northern and southern Europe in educational terms, in fact NEETs are concentrated in Andalusia, south Italy and Dacia.

Pupils and students enrollled in education

This category provides the total number of students (independent from sex and age) enrolled in school. As the map shows, there is no discrepancy between northern and southern Europe, with the only exception of Deutschland, characterized by lower figures.

Healthcare


Physicians, nurses and medical volonteers played an heroic role during the pandemic. But, were they adequatly equipped against this dramatic scenario? We decided to investigate whether healthcare systems throughout european countries had appropriate resources or whether the shortage of equipment could have enforced the mortality by Covid-19. We enlarged the analyses considering the presence of respiratory diseases in the population. Let's scroll down and discover them!

Healthcare personnel

This category concerns the health care staff, the ‘manpower’ active in the health care sector (doctors, dentists, nurses, etc.). This contributing factor is one of the most uniform, showing a significant similarity between nations.

Hospital discharges for respiratory diseases

It indicates the number of people who left the hospital after having suffered from respiratory diseases. In continental Europe, the distribution of the variable is quite homogeneous, Sweden instead is the region with the highest discharges for respiratory diseases.

Available Hospital Beds

It is the number of available hospital beds in a region. France and Belgium are characterised by a disposal noticeably larger than other European regions.

Long term care beds for hundred thousand people

It shows the number of long-term beds available in a region for every 100.000 inhabitants. Bulgaria possesses an average which is significantly higher than the other European regions.

Economy


Managing the pandemic implied managing the trade-off between economy and health. Exploring economic variables is important to investigate whether production activities could have been a vehicle of virulent diffusion due to the mobility and contact among employees. The European landscape is characterised by many industrial districts and professional hubs, dishomogeneously distributed: scroll down and look at them!

Gross domestic product (GDP)

The map describes the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) at current market prices. The pattern shown exhibits the lowest figures in eastern Europe, whereas strong peaks in the Scandinavian region and the northern part of the continental regions.

Compensation per employee

This map shows the sum of the compensations of all the employees of a certain area. The largest remunerations for employees are funded in the northern part of Europe, particularly in Luxembourg, Sweden and Denmark.

Unemployment rate

It reports the unemployment rate in a region. Europe is known for the strong economic inequalities that are spread around the continent, especially between the northern part and the Mediterranean one. This contributing factor in fact, stresses this phenomenon, exhibiting the largest rates in Spain and south Italy.

Thousands of hours worked

It shows the sum of the hours worked by employees in a certain area. The distribution of this contributing factor is quite uniform in the whole continent, except for the Luxembourg case, where the highest value is achieved and it is significantly larger than the average.

Real growth rate of regional gross value

GVA is an indicator of the economic activity of a country or a region. It reflects the total value of all goods and services produced less the value of goods and services used for intermediate consumption in their production. It is expressed as percentage change on the previous period. Such contributing factor reveals how much the economy of a country is growing compared to the previous year. Thus, the map enables to detect the fastest-growing nations, that are Romania and Estonia.

Population


A secret path that could lead to the explanation of different mortality and diffusion of Covid-19 deals with population. Since that virus is transmitted via air, the distribution and composition of citizens around Europe could generate valuable insights. Exploring demographic variables can detect pure relationships between Covid and local regions, since governors couldn't regulated them, except for social distance rules. So, scroll down and have a look!

Life expectancy

It represents the life expectancy for a person. The greatest values are in the north of Italy, with an average around 84.3 years, and the central part of Spain, showing the highest life expectancy in Europe with 85.8 years.

Population density

It indicates the density of population in each region. This contributing factor highlights a strong inhomogeneous pattern across European nations, underlying the distribution of urban areas, that largelt affect the local population density.

Crude death rate

It describes mortality in relation to the total population. Expressed in deaths per 100,000 inhabitants, it is calculated as the number of deaths recorded in the population for a given period divided by population in the same period and then multiplied by 100,000. The regions that present the largest values in death rate are concentrated in eastern Europe and Lithuania.

Deaths

It shows the number of deaths in each region. This pattern shows a similar distribution to the death crude rate one, in fact the largest figures are mainly present in the eastern Europe.

Mobility


Mobility has been strongly restricted by governors during the pandemic: only some types of journeys were allowed and the capacity of public transport was heavily reduced. The role of mobility is significantly relevant in the diffusion process, both for the quantity of people in the same closed environment and for the international movements of potential infected travellers allowing Covid-19 to become global. Scroll down and get some insights!

Air passengers

The map displays the number of passengers carried, in that region, per thousand of people. The distribution is really wide, ranging from 0.43 to 8581.8, showing a dissimilar behaviour across Europe.

Stocks of vehicles

This contributing factor reports the total number of vehicles present in a region. This contributing factor highlights a distribution difference between continental Europe and southern Europe: Italy and Portugal (the highest in the series) show a significant degree of vehicles, whereas Germany is characterised by the lowest record.

Primary sector


Covid-19 is a virus that spreads out by air: data about the primary sector are relevant since agricultural and breeding activities produce natural gases that can facilitate the virulent diffusion. Let's scroll down and discover primary sector in Europe!

Employed Agricultural Areas

The map represents the share of the utilized agricultural area (UAA) occupied by the main agricultural land uses (arable land, permanent grassland and land under permanent crops). The variety of the geographic territories in the European continent is mirrored by the distribution of this contributing factors, highlighting interesting differences between nations: Spain and Portugal achieve the largest utilized surface in the whole continent.

Farm Labour Force

It is expressed in persons and in Annual Work Units (AWU) where one AWU corresponds to the work performed by one person occupied on a full-time basis. The employment of individuals in the primary sector is especially diffused in the eastern regions of Europe, like Bulgaria and Romania, characterised by a significant gradient with respect to northern areas, due to the differences related to the economy and geographic territory.
