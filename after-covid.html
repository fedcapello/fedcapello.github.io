After Covid-19

Policies

Since March 2020, European governments have implemented a wide variety of Public Health and Social measurements in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. Which were the most effective choices? Scroll down and explore how Europe behaved in its fight against Covid-19!

Restrictions and regulations


As shown in the results, we tested the efficiency of several non-pharmacological policies that have been implemented across Europe during the first wave of the pandemic, showing that some of them proved to be more useful than others. The visualisations below show the percentages of days in which each type of policy have been active in a specific region during the first wave. Feel free to explore them all, and use the interactive visualisation to further investigate anti-COVID-19 policies!

Lockdown

Targets of the policy are obliged shelter in place irrespective of potential likelihood of COVID-19 transmission and are only allowed to leave their shelter for specific reasons. From the map, countries of southern Europe are the ones that spent the majority of time in lockdown.

Closure and Regulation of Schools

It regulates educational establishments in a country. This may include: closing an educational institution completely, allowing an educational institution to open with certain conditions; allowing an educational institution to stay open without conditions. Majority of European countries adopted this policy, except for Poland and Balcanic ones.

External Border Restrictions

It reduces the ability to access ports of entry or exit to or from different governmental jurisdictions. Almost all European countries adopted this policy, to reduce the cases brought from outside the borders.

Curfew

It limits domestic freedom of movement to certain times of the day. Few countries adopted this policy during the first wave. To be considered that during this wave, majority of the regions were under a complete lockdown, meaning that there was no need of curfew because was already incorporated in the lockdown policy.

Health Testing

It seeks to sample large populations for coronavirus regardless of suspected likelihood of affliction with COVID-19. Italy and Germany hugely implemented this policy, while other countries relied less on it, such as France or Austria.

Internal Border Restrictions

It reduces the ability to move freely within the borders of the initiating government. Italy relied heavily on this policy, since the outbreak developed in some regions before others. For other countries, especially in central Europe, they decided not to apply internal border restrictions, but to rely just on the external ones. It it although important to compare this map with the one of the lockdown embeds this policy.

Quarantine

Targets of the policy are obliged to isolate themselves for at least 14 days because there is reason to suspect a person is infected with COVID-19. Almost all countries have been applying this policy, except for the Iberian Peninsula, which relied less on it.

Restriction and Regulation of Government Services

It restricts and regulates government services, both essential and not essential. This can include ceasing government services completely; allowing government services to operate with conditions; or allowing government services to operate without conditions. All countries belonging to southern Europe relied on this policy, applying home working and digitalizing services. This happened less in central and eastern Europe.

Restrictions of Mass Gatherings

They limit the number of people allowed to congregate in a place. Almost all Europe relied on it. since the first cause of spred of the virus is the closeness between people.

Restriction and Regulation of Businesses

It regulates private, commercial activity. This can include closing down commercial establishments completely; allowing commercial establishments to open with conditions; or allowing commercial establishments to open without conditions. All countries belonging to western Europe relied on this policy, only some countries of eastern Europe implemented it for less time.

Social Distancing

It limits physical contact between individuals in public spaces. Almost all countries in Europe implemented this regulation, as it was one of the most simple and effective way to reduce the spread of the virus.

Our sources

The data used in our visualizations and analyses is public: all the data were retrieved from Coronanet, a database on government responses to the COVID-19 pandemic.
