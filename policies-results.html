Which anti-COVID-19 policies
did prove to be relevant?

By confronting national policies from several European countries, and misuring their impact on the number of cases, we managed to find the most effective type of regulations against the spread of COVID-19. Scroll down to find out which ones!

Policies analysis


In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, European governments have enacted a wide variety of Public Health and Social Measures (PHSM) since March 2020: despite similar in definition, they were inhomogeneously implemented across European regions, both in modality, intensity and time frame. In the fight against the virus, it is a priority to learn about the benefits of different measures to design effective exit strategies.

This section will help you navigate among the different policies of European countries, discovering their dissimilar time applications and real impact against the Covid-19 diffusion to finally achieve an evaluation of the effectiveness of such measures.

The figure above depicts a heatmap representing the percentage of days for which each policy type (y axis) has been active in each region (x axis) during the first wave (30/01/2020 - 20/08/2020): a color closer to red shows a longer usage.

It is possible to notice that regions belonging to the same country adopted a similar behaviour with regards to the application of the considered policies; this suggests that, when dealing with anti-contagion measures, we can refer to the national level rather than the regional one. For example, Italy and Poland confirm the homogeneity of measures inside the respective borders, but exhibit very distant strategies, with the former showing a much stricter response than the latter. Interestingly, Curfew has been rarely adopted, as opposed to External Border Restrictions, which were widely applied.



Each point in the figure represents a European country and its position depends on the adopted policies strategy during the first wave; the three different shapes are the result of a clustering technique (k-means), which groups together countries that displayed a similar behaviour.

As expected, Italy and Poland are at the extremes of the graph and are found to belong to distinct groups, while Luxemburg and Greece belong to the same group and are placed very closely.



This figure shows the average impact of each policy type (y axis) on the virus spread from 7 to 20 days (x axis) after its introduction, during the first wave: colder colors represent a deceleration of the curve, while warmer colors represent its acceleration. In general, most policies have a positive impact in limiting the virus spread during the second week (7-13 days) after the policy introduction.


The most effective measures, as shown by the graph, are the Restriction and Regulation of Businesses and the Internal Border Restrictions, followed by the Lockdown, which takes more time to reach its peak effectiveness in the third week after its introduction. Surprisingly, the Closure and Regulation of Schools and especially the Quarantine, seem to have much lower impact than expected. Explore our data

Are you interested in finding out more on how anti-COVID-19 policies helped to stop the virus? Download our dataset to learn what happened near you and around the continent.

Our data are entirely open source, feel free to dowload it and use it for your own analysis and research. Our aim is to make information available to the public for free, in order build free and reliable knowledge on this ongoing pandemic. For further information, you can also download our report, to find out more about our research.

Our sources

The data used in our visualizations and analyses is public: all the data were retrieved from Coronanet, a database on government responses to the COVID-19 pandemic.
